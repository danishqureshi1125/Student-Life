Student Resources

Helpful academic, wellness, and productivity resources for college students.

Resource Center

College can be stressful when students are trying to manage classes, assignments, exams, work schedules, and personal responsibilities. This page brings together practical resources students can use to stay organized, get support, and build better habits.

Academic Support

Use academic support when class material becomes difficult or confusing.

  • Visit professor office hours for assignment or exam questions.
  • Join a study group with classmates.
  • Use tutoring services for difficult subjects.
  • Review lecture notes within 24 hours after class.

Study Tools

These tools can help students study more effectively.

  • Quizlet for flashcards and practice review.
  • Google Docs for shared notes.
  • Pomodoro timers for focused study sessions.
  • Practice tests for active recall.

Time Management

Planning ahead makes deadlines easier to manage.

  • Use Google Calendar to track exams and due dates.
  • Create a weekly checklist every Sunday or Monday.
  • Break large assignments into smaller tasks.
  • Set reminders at least one day before deadlines.

Mental Health Support

Students should ask for help early when stress builds up.

  • Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or advisor.
  • Use campus counseling or wellness services if available.
  • Take breaks during long study sessions.
  • Do not wait until burnout gets worse.

Productivity Apps

Apps can help students stay organized and focused.

  • Notion for notes, planning, and organization.
  • Microsoft To Do for daily task lists.
  • Google Calendar for scheduling.
  • Forest or Pomodoro apps for focus sessions.

Healthy Habits

Small habits can improve energy, focus, and motivation.

  • Get enough sleep before exams.
  • Drink water throughout the day.
  • Take short walks or stretch breaks.
  • Eat balanced meals when possible.

Student Success Tip

The best resource is the one you actually use consistently. Start with one habit, such as using a weekly planner or attending office hours, and build from there.
